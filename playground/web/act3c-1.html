Trees

by: Joyce Kilmer

I

think

that I

shall never see

A poem lovely as a tree.


A tree whose hungry mouth

is pressed against the earth's sweet

flowing breast, a tree that looks at God

all day and lifts her leafy arms to pray;


A tree that may in Summer wear a nest of robins

in her hair; Upon whose bosom snow has lain;

Who intimately lives with rain.

Poems

are

made

by

fools

like

me,

But

only

God

can

make a tree.
